Key Factors Affecting Academic Performance

Project Objective

Analyze the factors influencing students' academic performance through statistical and predictive models, identifying key variables such as study time, absences, and parental support.

Justification

Understanding these factors allows educational institutions, parents, and teachers to make informed decisions to improve academic outcomes, focusing resources on the most impactful aspects.

Methodology

Data Used

  • Educational dataset from Kaggle containing information on 2,382 students
  • Key variables: GPA, weekly study time, absences, parental support
  • Secondary variables: tutoring, extracurricular activities, parental education

Analytical Process

  1. Data cleaning: Filtering relevant variables
  2. Exploratory analysis: Descriptive statistics and correlation matrices
  3. Predictive modeling: Linear regression to predict GPA
  4. Visualization: Coefficient plots and heatmaps

Results

Parental Support

Positive correlation with GPA (r = 0.19)

Strongest positive impact in the model

Absences

Strong negative correlation (r = -0.32)

Significant negative effect on GPA

Study Time

Weak positive correlation (r = 0.18)

Less impact than expected

Validated Hypotheses

  • H1: Parental support has a positive effect on GPA (p < 0.01)
  • H2: Absences significantly reduce GPA (p < 0.001)
  • H3: Study time shows positive but less significant effect (p = 0.03)
